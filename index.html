Press First

New in v6 — NFL-Style Tactics Installation
THE PLAYBOOK IS
INSTALLED. NOT ASSUMED.

Professional football teams don't install their full system on Day 1. They use a proven two-phase structure — Organized Team Activities to install concepts without full pressure, then Training Camp to pressure-test every tactic at full speed. We use the exact same structure. Tactics are layered in, earned rep by rep, and owned before they're deployed in a match.

OTA Period — Weeks 1–6
Organized Team
Activities

No full-speed opposition. No punishment for mistakes. One concept per session — installed with teaching, not conditioning. Think of it like the NFL where quarterbacks can't be sacked during OTAs. The point is learning, not winning.

Week 1
Identity Install
3 principles. 4-3-3 shape. DM landmarks. Walk-through only — no ball. Players write their landmarks in their notebook.
Week 2
Build-Out + Possession
GK build-out sequence. 4v2 Rondo. Shape walk twice at walking pace. No defenders present.
Week 3
DM Role + Pivot
DM owns the whiteboard. Scanning, switching, pivoting — without defensive pressure. CMs learn their support angles.
Week 4
Press Trigger — Concept
"PRESS!" trigger word introduced. All 11 walk to their press positions. Timed at jog pace only. No full execution yet.
Week 5
Set Pieces — ALPHA through DELTA
Four named plays installed as walk-throughs. Corner, free kick roles assigned. Decoy runs rehearsed. No GK yet.
Week 6
OTA Review + Depth Chart
Full shape walk. Landmark comprehension graded. Players who know every landmark earn first-team reps in camp.
🏕️
Training Camp — Weeks 7–10
Full Speed.
Full Contact.

The install is done. Now it gets pressure-tested. Full speed, live opposition, competitive depth chart. Mistakes cost reps. Players who execute earn more time. Every single rep counts.

Week 7
Camp Day 1 — Identity at Speed
All OTA drills run at full pace for the first time. First live 11v11. Anyone who doesn't know their landmarks goes to scout team.
Week 8
Live Press Execution
GK trap press and mid-block press run at full speed for the first time. Counterpress added. First time the press actually breaks down under pressure — coach notes, doesn't stop play.
Week 9
Live Set Pieces + 2nd Formation
All six named plays rehearsed with live GK and defenders. 4-2-3-1 installed as alternative shape. Two-a-day schedule begins.
Week 10
Scrimmage + Depth Chart Final
Full 90-minute scrimmage vs external opponent. All tactics live. Individual install reports delivered to every player.
Two-A-Day Format — Week 9
AM session: technical polish + set piece choreography. PM session: live 11v11 + spot kicks under fatigue. Same structure as NFL camp — peak volume, full recovery protocol between sessions.
Player Install Report Cards
EVERY PLAYER
GETS GRADED.

At the end of OTA Week 6 and Camp Week 10, every player receives a personal install report card. Not a talent evaluation — a tactical knowledge and execution grade. 5 questions at OTA, 5 execution grades at Camp. Scoring determines first-team reps.

OTA Week 6 — Knowledge Test
5/5 = First team. 4/5 = Starter with note. 3/5 = Rotation. 2/5 or below = Scout team until re-test passed.
Camp Week 10 — Execution Grade
Coach grades from film: press timing, set piece role, build-out decisions, tactical IQ. Any player who can't name their growth area goes back to OTA concepts.
Rep Target System
Every tactic has a rep target before it's "installed": 4-3-3 shape = 50 reps. Press trigger = 30 team reps. Named corner plays = 20 live reps each. No tactic is called "installed" before the target is hit.
