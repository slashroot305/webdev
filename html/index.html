16-Week Off-Season Plan — Included in Every Package
TWO PROGRAMS.
ONE SYSTEM.

Most players spend their off-season losing what they gained. This 16-week plan is built specifically around the physical demands of the NFSA program — so every player returns fitter, stronger, and faster than they left.

The only question is whether your players are going to build in the off-season — or fall behind.

16

4

6

01
Recovery & Reset — Weeks 1–3
Active recovery, mobility restoration, structural prep. No skipping this. Players who skip Block 1 peak early and fade in Month 5.
02
Strength Foundation — Weeks 4–8
Trap bar deadlift, Nordic hamstring, hip thrust, Bulgarian split squat. Builds the physical base for aerial duels, 1v1s, and hold-up play.
03
Power & Speed — Weeks 9–13
Sprint mechanics, plyometrics, repeated sprint capacity. Targets: 0–10m under 1.80s · 30m under 4.10s · Vertical jump over 55cm.
04
Pre-Season Ramp — Weeks 14–16
Shift from athlete to footballer. Reconnect to the ball. Arrive at Week 1 of the in-season program sharp, fit, and ready to compete immediately.
What the Program Physically Demands — And How the Off-Season Builds It
⚡
Explosive Speed
Press triggers and 1v1 winger isolation require 0–10 yard burst acceleration. Overspeed training, resistance sprints, and plyometric progressions build the fast-twitch base during the off-season.
🏋️
Functional Strength
Aerial duels, hold-up play, and 1v1 physicality demand whole-body strength. Compound lifts — squat, hip hinge, pull — build soccer-specific power without bulk.
🫀
Aerobic Engine
Box-to-box midfielders and fullbacks cover 8–11km per match. Zone 2 base work, lactate threshold intervals, and repeated sprint capacity training build the engine to last 90 minutes.
🦵
Injury Resilience
High-intensity year-round play creates overuse risk. Targeted hip, ankle, and knee stability work — plus hamstring loading protocols — reduce injury risk before it ever happens.
🔁
Change of Direction
Pressing, covering, and dribbling all require rapid direction changes. Lateral mechanics, deceleration training, and agility pattern work that transfers directly from the gym to the pitch.
🧠
Mental Resilience
High-tempo soccer demands composure under fatigue. Visualization protocols, pressure inoculation drills, identity language, and performance journaling — the mental edge is trained, not inherited.
