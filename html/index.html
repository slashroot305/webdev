NEW — Off-Season System

The off-season
is where champions
are built.

Most players spend their off-season losing what they gained. This 16-week off-season plan is built specifically around the physical and mental demands of the NFSA program — so every player returns fitter, stronger, faster, and mentally sharper than they left.

Structured across four blocks: Recovery & Reset, Strength Foundation, Power & Speed, and Pre-Season Ramp. Both individual and team tracks included.

Physical Foundation

What the program physically demands — and how the off-season builds it

⚡
Explosive Speed
High-press triggers and 1v1 winger isolation require 0–10 yard burst acceleration. Off-season: overspeed training, resistance sprints, and plyometric progressions build the fast-twitch base.
🏋️
Functional Strength
Aerial duels, hold-up play, and physical 1v1 battles demand whole-body strength. Off-season: compound lifts (squat, hip hinge, pull) build soccer-specific power without bulk.
🫀
Aerobic Engine
Box-to-box midfielders and fullbacks run 8–11km per match. Off-season: zone 2 aerobic base work, lactate threshold intervals, and repeated sprint capacity training.
🦵
Injury Resilience
High-intensity year-round play creates overuse risk. Off-season: targeted hip, ankle, and knee stability work. Hamstring loading protocols. Reduce injury risk before it happens.
🔁
Change of Direction
Pressing, covering, and dribbling all require rapid direction changes. Off-season: lateral mechanics, deceleration training, and agility pattern work that transfers directly to the pitch.
🧠
Mental Resilience
High-intensity, high-tempo soccer demands composure under fatigue. Off-season: mindset work, visualization protocols, pressure inoculation — so players perform when it matters most.

Mental Performance

The mental edge is trained — not inherited

Physical fitness without mental resilience creates players who perform in training and disappear in matches. This system trains both.

🎯
Visualization Protocol
10 minutes daily, eyes closed. Players mentally rehearse their position-specific skills at perfect quality. Research shows mental rehearsal activates the same neural pathways as physical practice. Do this every morning during the off-season.
😤
Pressure Inoculation
Drills designed to be performed under accumulated fatigue — simulating the mental pressure of the 80th minute. Finishing sessions always end with a shooting challenge when players are already tired. Train what you'll feel in the match.
💬
Identity Language
Coaches use specific language to build identity. "We press immediately." "We win our 1v1s." "We play forward first." Repeated consistently, these become automatic beliefs that drive behavior under pressure without thinking.
📓
Performance Journaling
Weekly journal entries for each player: What did I do well this week? What do I want to improve? What is my goal next week? Takes 5 minutes. Builds self-awareness, ownership, and a measurable improvement mindset.
🛌
Sleep Optimization
Sleep is the single biggest performance variable for ages 12–19. Target 8–9 hours. Coaches communicate sleep expectations to families. Athletes who sleep well adapt faster, recover faster, and make better decisions in-game.
🔥
Resilience Reframing
After every mistake in training: players say out loud what they should have done instead. Eliminates lingering on errors and builds a growth mindset. Coaches never repeat negative feedback — replace with correct behavior reinforcement immediately.
🥗
Nutrition & Fueling Guidelines — Included
The off-season plan includes a complete youth athlete nutrition framework. Carbohydrate timing for training days vs rest days. Protein targets by body weight (ages 12–19). Pre-match meal protocols. Hydration benchmarks. Recovery nutrition windows. The food decisions players make off the pitch are as important as what they do on it.

Defensive DNA

Press like
the NFL.

Four complete pressing systems, each with a trigger, an execution pattern, and a progressive drill program. Built on the same defensive frameworks that NFL coordinators spend careers designing.

NFL: Zone Blitz
Gegenpress
Counter-Press
3 nearest players collapse in a triangle on the ball-carrier within 2 seconds of any turnover. Block all escape routes simultaneously. The most dangerous transition moments become your most reliable attacking opportunities.
Trigger word: "PRESS!" — Any turnover in attacking or middle third
NFL: Tampa 2
4-4-2
Mid-Block
Two compact banks of four slide collectively toward the ball. Strikers cut the central pass to the DM. Force the wide pass, then spring a coordinated double-team trap on the receiving fullback. Controlled, zonal, suffocating.
Trigger word: "HOLD!" — Opponent building slowly from the back
NFL: Man Coverage
Man-Oriented
High Press
Each outfield player is assigned one opponent to shadow and press. Every passing option is blanketed simultaneously. Maximum collective pressure — no outlet, no time, no comfort. High risk, devastating when fit enough to execute.
Trigger: Coach signals — Used Month 4+ only. Requires peak fitness.
NFL: Cover 0 Overload
Trap Press
Ambush
Deliberately open one side to invite the pass, then spring a 3-player overload ambush. The opponent finds what looks like space — and walks into a designed trap. This is championship-level defensive intelligence.
Trigger word: "TRAP!" — Protecting a lead or forcing a goal late

Center
Backs
The defensive foundation. Aerial dominance, calm distribution under pressure, and commanding box leadership. A great CB prevents goals before they're ever attempted.
Aerial Dominance Ball Distribution 1v1 Defending Communication Positioning
01
Aerial Dominance Circuit
Attack every cross before the striker. Headed clearances, box positioning, and physical competition for high balls. Win every aerial duel by claiming space before the opponent moves.
02
Distribution Under Press
Receive from GK with a runner pressing immediately. Play accurately to FB or switch to far CB in 1-2 touches. Build composure that never cracks under real match pressure.
03
1v1 Defending (Attacker Drives)
Set the stance, jockey, delay, then time the tackle or force wide. Never ball-watch. Stay between attacker and goal. Patience used as a weapon.
04
Ball-Playing CB Circuit
Drive with the ball, find the DM, switch play, and play vertical passes through lines under increasing pressure. The modern CB is the 12th midfielder.
Full
backs
The engine on both sides of the pitch. Attack like a winger, defend like a CB. The most physically demanding role in the modern game demands a specialist program.
Overlap TimingCrossing DeliveryRecovery Runs1v1 Containing
01
Overlap Sprint Timing
CM plays wide, FB sprints past winger to receive the overlap. Must arrive before the defender recovers. Cross or shoot. Every rep timed.
02
1v1 Containment
Delay. Show inside. Time the challenge. Contain first, steal second. Win by positioning — not desperation.
03
3-Type Crossing Delivery
Near post drive, far post curl, low pullback. 3-touch limit. Quality of delivery judged — not quantity.
04
Diagonal Recovery Run
Pushed forward — ball switches sides — sprint 40 yards diagonally back before winger receives. The fitness test disguised as a tactical drill.
Defensive
Mid
The heartbeat of the team. Scan everything. Intercept before others react. Distribute with one touch. Press-resistant, intelligent, and always available.
360° ScanningInterceptionsPress ResistanceLong Switch Pass
01
360° Scanning Drill
Coach shows direction cards while DM receives passes. Must call the card before passing on. Trains pre-scanning as a muscle memory habit — not a conscious effort.
02
Interception Positioning
Read the pass before it happens. Position BEFORE the ball is played — not react after. Interceptions won through intelligence, not athleticism.
03
Press Resistance Rondo 3v1
Three players press immediately on every touch. DM controls, turns, finds the right pass within 1.5 seconds. Maximum pressure. 90-second rep.
04
Long Switch Distribution
Receive under pressure, switch play to isolated wide player in 2 touches. Both feet. Driven — not floated. Pin-point at pace, every time.
Central
Mids
Box-to-box engines. Read play two passes ahead. Arrive late in the box. Link defense to attack in one touch. The most complete athletes on the pitch.
Third Man RunsBox ArrivalCombination PlayEngine
01
Third Man Run Circuit
P1→P2, P2 lays to CM who drives forward into the box for a shot. Read the game 2 passes ahead. Intelligence + fitness in one drill.
02
Box-to-Box Shuttle Passing
Two CMs pass through center, box to box — 60-yard runs with ball ending in a shot on goal. The engine test disguised as a technical drill.
03
1-2-3 Combination Patterns
CM + winger + striker in rotating patterns at the edge of the box ending in driven shots. Executed at full match tempo — no slowing down.
04
Late Box Run
Winger receives wide, CM begins blind-side run. Winger delivers at the precise moment. CM finishes first time. It's about timing — not raw speed.
Wing
ers
Beat your man. That's the job. Receive in space, face up the defender, and go. This program turns technically capable wingers into game-breakers that scouts actively track.
1v1 DribblingSpeed with BallCrossing & CuttingExplosive First Step
01
Speed Dribbling Gates
Control the ball at speed through cone gates at 5, 10, 15, and 20 yards. Target: 20 yards with ball in under 4 seconds. Develops ball control at elite pace.
02
1v1 Attack Sequence — 8 Min
8 minutes of consecutive 1v1s — new defender every 60 seconds. Win the duel then cross. Repetition builds 1v1 muscle memory that no defender can disrupt.

Two-Weapon Winger Training
Receive, play 1-2, then choose: drive and cross OR cut inside and shoot. Defenders can't know which. Both options equally trained, equally dangerous.
04
Explosive First Step
Light resistance band on first touch. Explode away against resistance — simulates shaking a tight defender. Develops pure acceleration from a standing start.
Strik
ers
Score goals. Hold the ball. Press from the front. Lead the line. Strikers in this program finish chances, create for others, and terrify defensive lines from minute one.
FinishingHold-Up PlayMovement PatternsPress from Front
01
Finishing Across 6 Zones
6 positions in and around the box. 3 shots from each = 18 per rep. Track success by zone. Improve the weakest zone each week. No comfort zones allowed.
02
Hold-Up Under Pressure
Ball to back, defender presses immediately. Control, hold, and lay off in under 3 seconds. Use the body as a shield. The skill that separates good strikers from great ones.
03
Movement Pattern Training
Coach calls: NEAR / FAR / DROP / SPIN — striker executes the run, ball delivered immediately. Train the movement vocabulary every elite striker must own.
04
Press from Front — Engine Test
Sprint 40 yards to close GK/CB, force a hasty clearance, recover, repeat. 90-second count. Strikers who press from the front win games their team shouldn't.
